
**How to Prune Basil for Better Growth”**
**如果你想更口语一点，也可以用：**
**“Getting Advice on Pruning My Basil Plant”**
**“Learning How to Care for My Basil”**
**“Fixing My Basil: When and Where to Cut”**

Me: Hey, I’ve been growing some basil lately, and I noticed something at the top of the plant. There are these really tiny leaves—should I let them grow or trim them?

Friend: Good question. If the plant is still small, like under 10 cm with only a few leaf pairs, let it grow. But if it already has around 4–6 pairs of leaves, it’s actually a good time to trim the top.

---

Me: Got it. I think mine is already a bit bigger. But I also see some small green fuzzy balls near the top—are those just new leaves?

Friend: Ah, those aren’t leaves. Those are flower buds, or at least the plant is about to flower.

---

Me: Oh, really? Is that bad?

Friend: Not ideal. Once basil starts flowering, the leaves lose flavor and the plant slows down. So you should remove those buds as soon as you see them.

---

Me: So I should just cut off the whole top?

Friend: Yes, but do it properly. Cut just above a pair of healthy leaves below the bud. That way, the plant will branch out from that point.

---

Me: Interesting. I also noticed one branch splits into three. The middle one has a bud right above two big leaves. Should I cut off that entire middle branch?

Friend: No, don’t remove the whole branch. Just cut off the bud and the tip. Leave those two big leaves—they’ll grow into two new branches.

---

Me: Okay, so only remove the tip, not the structure.

Friend: Exactly. Think of it this way: you’re trimming points, not removing branches.

---

Me: One more thing—the bud is really close to the leaves. Do I need to be super precise when cutting?

Friend: You don’t need surgical precision, but don’t cut too close either. Leave about half a centimeter to one centimeter above the leaf pair. That protects the growth points.

---

Me: So it’s better to cut slightly higher than risk damaging the leaves?

Friend: Yes. As long as the bud is gone and the leaves are intact, you’re good.

---

Me: Makes sense. Oh, and I noticed some tiny black spots on the leaves too…

Friend: That might be pests. Rinse the plant with water first, and if needed, use a very diluted soap solution. Deal with that before pruning, since new growth is more vulnerable.

---

Me: Got it. So: clean pests first, then prune the buds properly.

Friend: Exactly. If you keep pruning regularly, your basil will get bushier and produce way more leaves.

---

Me: Thanks! I feel like I’m starting to understand how this works now.

Friend: You are. Once you get the hang of pruning, basil becomes super productive.
